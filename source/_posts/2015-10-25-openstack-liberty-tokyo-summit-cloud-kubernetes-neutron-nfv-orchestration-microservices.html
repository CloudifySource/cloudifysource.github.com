It’s on, folks! Tokyo is not just around the corner, it’s here - and OpenStack peddlers, developers, fanboys, newbies will all be out there to hear about how everyone is using OpenStack to improve the open cloud initiative and push this amazing project forward.

Our own Cloudify team will also be making their way out east to the Land of the Rising Sun to showcase a few of the things we are working on in OpenStack. We will be talking about all things pure-play, open source orchestration - everything from NFV and TOSCA to microservices and Kubernetes.

We are also excited to announce our partnership with Mirantis in creating, and completing, a plugin for Murano that allows users to extend this formerly OpenStack-specific application catalog to any infrastructure, and even deploy and model applications on a hybrid infrastructure. More on that topic in an upcoming blog post.

Of course, a post about the OpenStack Summit would not be complete without a nice list of upcoming Cloudify news and activities, so here it is:

  • Cloudify 3.3 is on its way this quarter with some major updates to the Visual Composer, the addition of IPv6, and many other anticipated features. For more, check out the Cloudify 3.3 roadmap.

  • We now enable Kubernetes to be extended to OpenStack along with other clouds  including VMware

  • Cloudify 3.3 will support OpenStack LIberty.

  • Our software now supports mixed OpenStack, VMware, and Docker workloads so users can run apps on hybrid cloud environments.
